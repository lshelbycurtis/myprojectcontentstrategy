Seacrest Wolf Preservation

Content Strategy Proposal

Our Purpose

The purpose of the Seacrest Wolf Preserves website is to educate and engage visitors about the mission, services, and overall experience offered by the preserve. We aim to provide information about wolf conservation, promote visitor participation, and raise awareness about the importance of preserving wolves and their ecosystem.

Interactor profile

Audience

The intended target audience for our website includes individuals interested in wildlife conservation, nature enthusiasts, animal lovers, and families seeking educational and interactive experiences. These individuals are likely to be curious about wolves, passionate about conservation efforts, and interested in supporting organizations that prioritize environmental preservation.

AGE: The intended interactors are ages 10 and up.

GENDER: The target audience are any and all genders.

EDUCATION: The target audience is of all education levels.

CONTEXT: The target audience is urban, suburban and rural.

CULTURAL PERSPECTIVE: The intended interactors are from the United States, and they are of all cultures.

SOCIO-ECONOMIC: The intended interactors are from all socio-economic classes.

EXPERTISE: The target audience has a novice to expert level of knowledge about wolves.

ATTITUDES: The target audience has a neutral to positive attitude about wolves. Some may be neutral but have a general fear of wolves instilled by society. Others may have a love or passion for wolves and want to know more about a like-minded organization.

Frequency of publication

The content on our website needs to be updated regularly to ensure its relevance and keep visitors engaged. This includes updating information about visitor guidelines, upcoming events, educational programs, and recent research findings. By maintaining an up-to-date website, the preserve can demonstrate its commitment to providing accurate and timely information.

Information challenges

The audience needs to know specific details about the preserve's mission, educational programs, visitor guidelines, and the impact of their support. Additionally, it is crucial to communicate the importance of wolf conservation, their role in the ecosystem, and any ongoing initiatives or research conducted by the preserve.

One potential challenge is conveying this information quickly and clearly, as the subject matter may be complex or unfamiliar to some visitors. Using clear language, visual aids, and structured navigation can help overcome these obstacles.

Content Fitness

The information quality of the Seacrest Wolf Preserve website is generally good, but there is room for improvement. The website effectively communicates the preserve's mission and services, providing clear descriptions and engaging visuals. However, some sections could benefit from more detailed information, such as the ongoing research and initiatives the preserve is involved in. Moreover, incorporating user-generated content, such as visitor testimonials or success stories, could enhance the authenticity and impact of the website.

Intrinsic IQ:

Intrinsic IQ focuses on the ability of a website to engage and captivate its users through interactive and dynamic elements. To enhance the Intrinsic IQ of the Seacrest Wolf Preserve website, incorporating interactive features such as virtual tours or live webcams can provide visitors with a unique and immersive experience.

The website can also include a blog or news section to regularly update visitors on the latest events, research findings, or success stories related to the preserve. This can encourage repeat visits and keep users engaged with fresh and relevant content. Additionally, integrating social media feeds or user-generated content, such as photos or videos shared by visitors, can create a sense of community and encourage interaction among users.

Representational IQ:

Chapter 5 of "Writing and Editing for Digital Media" discusses the concept of Representational IQ, which refers to the ability of a website to effectively communicate its purpose and content to its users. In the case of the website, the Representational IQ can be evaluated by analyzing how well the website conveys information about the preserve's mission, services, and the overall experience it offers.

The homepage of the website should prominently feature a clear and concise description of the preserve's mission and purpose. It should showcase high-quality images and videos of the wolves and the preserve itself to visually engage visitors and give them a glimpse into what they can expect.

Additionally, the website should provide easy-to-find navigation menus and links to different sections, such as information about the preserve's history, educational programs, and visitor guidelines.

Accessibility IQ:

Accessibility IQ refers to the ability of a website to be easily accessible and usable by all users, regardless of their abilities or disabilities. To ensure a high Accessibility IQ for the Seacrest Wolf Preserve website, it should adhere to web accessibility standards, such as providing alt text for images, closed captioning for videos, and clear headings and navigation labels for screen reader users.

The website should also prioritize responsive design, making it accessible on various devices, including desktops, tablets, and smartphones. It should have adjustable text sizes and color contrast options to accommodate users with visual impairments. Furthermore, providing alternative ways to access information, such as downloadable brochures or transcripts for videos, can further enhance the accessibility of the website for all users.

Contextual IQ:

Contextual IQ, as discussed in Chapter 5, refers to the ability of a website to provide relevant and related information to its users. For the website of Seacrest Wolf Preserve, the Contextual IQ can be enhanced by including sections that provide additional information about the importance of wolf conservation, their role in the ecosystem, and any ongoing research or initiatives that the preserve is involved in.

The website should also include testimonials or stories from previous visitors to showcase the impact the preserve has had on them and why others should consider visiting. Furthermore, providing links or resources to other wildlife conservation organizations or related articles and research can help visitors gain a deeper understanding of the topic and its broader context.

Social Media Presence

The website includes a blog that can be used to provide regular updates, educational content, and stories related to the preserve. However, the blog could be utilized more effectively to meet the audience's needs by incorporating more interactive elements and encouraging visitor engagement. The social media presence of Seacrest Wolf Preserve, including Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram accounts, is appropriate and regularly updated. These platforms provide an opportunity to share captivating visuals, educational content, and engage with the audience through comments and discussions.

The competition

Organizations competing for the same audience as the Seacrest Wolf Preserve include wildlife conservation organizations, animal sanctuaries, and nature reserves. Websites such as the Wolf Conservation Center (https://nywolf.org/) and International Wolf Center (https://www.wolf.org/) offer similar educational content and advocate for wolf conservation. Comparing these websites to the Seacrest Wolf Preserve's website can provide insights into areas for improvement, such as additional educational resources, interactive features, or the integration of visitor testimonials.

Similarities

  • All three websites are dedicated to the preservation and conservation of wolves.

  • They provide information about the different species of wolves and their habitats.

  • They educate the public about the importance of wolves in ecosystems and their role as apex predators.

  • All websites offer opportunities for visitors to learn more through educational programs, events, and resources.

  • They have sections dedicated to wolf research and scientific studies.

  • Each website provides ways for visitors to get involved and support their conservation efforts through donations, memberships, and volunteer opportunities.

Differences

  • The Seacrest Wolf Preservation's website focuses on the rescue and rehabilitation of captive wolves, while the Wolf Conservation Center's website primarily concentrates on the recovery and reintroduction of endangered wolf species.

  • The International Wolf Center's website has a broader global focus and provides information about wolf conservation efforts worldwide, while the other two websites primarily focus on initiatives in their respective regions.

  • The Seacrest Wolf Preservation's website has a strong emphasis on education, with resources specifically designed for teachers and students, while the other two websites also cater to the general public.

  • The Wolf Conservation Center's website offers live webcams that allow visitors to observe wolves in real-time, while the other websites do not have this feature.

  • The International Wolf Center's website has a dedicated section for wolf tracking, providing updates on collared wolves and their movements, whereas the other two websites do not focus on tracking individual wolves.

  • The design and layout of the websites vary, with each organization having its own unique style and navigation system.

Web Presence

To address the information challenges, the organization should consider expanding the website's content to provide more in-depth information about ongoing research, conservation initiatives, and success stories. This could be achieved through regular updates in the blog section or by creating dedicated pages for specific projects. Additionally, integrating user-generated content, such as visitor testimonials or photos, can enhance authenticity and engage the audience.

Social Media

The organization's social media presence is strong, but there is room for improvement. The Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram accounts should be leveraged to share captivating visuals, educational content, and promote visitor engagement. However, the organization could benefit from establishing a presence on platforms that cater to nature enthusiasts and wildlife conservation, such as Pinterest or YouTube. These platforms would allow the organization to showcase visual content, share educational videos, and reach a wider audience.

Readership

To increase readership on social media sites and redirect users to the website, the organization should adopt a multi-channel approach. This can be achieved by cross-promoting content across different platforms, encouraging followers to visit the website for more detailed information. The blog can be utilized to create informative and engaging content that is shareable on social media platforms, directing users back to the website for further exploration. Additionally, the organization can collaborate with influencers or partner with other conservation organizations to reach new audiences and drive traffic to the website.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Seacrest Wolf Preserve can strengthen its content strategy by enhancing the website's information quality, improving interactivity on the blog, and optimizing the social media presence. By providing more detailed information, incorporating user-generated content, and expanding the organization's social media presence, the preserve can engage its target audience more effectively. Implementing a multi-channel approach and encouraging cross-promotion between social media sites and the website will help increase readership and redirect users to the website, ultimately promoting the organization's mission of wolf conservation and environmental preservation.
